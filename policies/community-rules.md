# Community Rules

**Status:** DRAFT — UNAPPROVED  
**Effective when:** counsel + product + T&S accept; until then STAGING/INTERNAL only  
**Updated:** 2026-07-21

Adults only (18+). These rules are **behavior-focused**. They are not a “no rules” service and not a vague “offensive speech” code.

## Consent and contact

- Private messaging begins only after a **mutual match**, except tightly rate-limited, non-media interest signals the product explicitly allows.
- Stop contact immediately after block, unmatched withdrawal of consent, or a clear “do not contact.”
- Do not coerce, pressure, shame, threaten, or retaliate against someone who declines, blocks, reports, leaves a group, changes intent, or revokes a profile/location grant.
- Consent to proximity detection, profile disclosure, sexual intent, a meeting, a group encounter, media, or location sharing are separate decisions. One never implies another.
- A match is not consent to sex, location access, physical contact, recording, or sharing intimate media.

## Prohibited conduct

### Child safety and age

- No person under 18 may create, access, appear in, or be targeted through the dating service.
- Prohibited: age evasion, grooming, child sexual abuse/exploitation material (CSAM/CSAE), sexualization of minors, sextortion involving minors, or helping a minor obtain adult-service access.
- Proximity, sexual-intent, group, map, marketplace-social, messaging, and matching features are adult-only.

### Trafficking and commercial sexual exploitation

- Prohibited: trafficking, coercion, recruitment for exploitation, pimping, and unlawful commercial-sex solicitation.
- The service and Skin Shop must not be used to promote, arrange, advertise, or compensate sexual services.

### Intimate imagery and NCII

- Prohibited: nonconsensual intimate imagery (NCII), hidden-camera content, sexual content involving an incapacitated person, threats to distribute intimate material, or sharing a match’s intimate preferences without permission.
- Qualifying NCII removal requests follow the published human-owned process and applicable legal timelines.

### Proximity, stalking, location, and privacy abuse

- Prohibited: stalking, doxxing, disclosing exact location, coercing location access, following a person after a proximity alert, or contact after block.
- Do not harvest, replay, correlate, sell, or publish Bluetooth encounter identifiers.
- Do not use modified clients, scanners, antennas, relay attacks, or venue sweeps to identify or count nearby users.
- Do not reveal that another person selected a sexual or relationship intent unless that person’s disclosure policy permits it.
- Do not pressure another person to enable **Get fk'd**, automatic profile sharing, a meeting pin, or live location.
- A shared location may be used only for the purpose for which it was granted and only until expiry or revocation.

### Group encounters

- Every participant must be an eligible adult and must see and accept the complete participant list.
- Adding or replacing a participant requires renewed consent from everyone.
- Do not expose another participant’s profile, intent, media, or location to a group without that participant’s explicit grant.
- Leaving, blocking, or revoking consent must end that person’s participation and access immediately.

### Violence and threats

- Prohibited: credible threats, incitement of imminent violence, extortion, kidnapping, instructions tied to an active target, coercive control, or weaponized location sharing.

### Fraud, bots, and inauthentic behavior

- Prohibited: impersonation, romance scams, financial fraud, malicious links, credential theft, spam, bot farms, profile farms, coordinated inauthentic behavior, automated mass liking, scraping, fake matches, or bypassing rate limits.
- Do not sell or transfer accounts, adult-eligibility credentials, device attestations, marketplace entitlements, or creator identities.
- Automated agents must be explicitly authorized, labeled, isolated from dating discovery, and unable to contact users unless a separate approved product mode permits it.

### Skin Shop and creator conduct

- Prohibited: stolen artwork, copyright/trademark infringement, impersonation assets, malicious files, hidden network behavior, executable code in skins, deceptive previews, fake purchases, refund fraud, payout fraud, or laundering funds through digital assets.
- Avatars must not falsely imply photo verification, identity verification, professional credentials, or affiliation.
- Creator, purchaser, or spending status must not improve dating rank, visibility, message delivery, report priority, or safety access.

### Platform integrity

- Prohibited: malware or attempts to bypass adult eligibility, attestation, bot protection, abuse prevention, profile disclosure, location grants, group consent, block/report, or child-safety controls.
- Do not reverse engineer another person’s private questionnaire answers or modify a client to obtain answers marked “score only.”

## Public profiles and distribution fitness

Public profiles and Skin Shop assets must remain compatible with distribution requirements:

- no pornographic primary purpose;
- no explicit public nudity as the product’s purpose;
- no anonymous/random-chat positioning;
- no public “hot-or-not” / attractiveness scoring;
- no public sexual-intent broadcasting;
- objectionable public material must be filterable and reportable.

Swipes are **private preference signals**, never public ratings. `Looking For` selections and sensitive questionnaire answers are private by default.

## Identity, gender, orientation, and discovery preferences

- Users may privately choose which gender identities they want in their own candidate feed.
- Gender identity, pronouns, sexual orientation, who-I-see, and who-may-see-me are separate optional settings.
- Do not harass, degrade, threaten, or target people based on gender identity, sexual orientation, race, ethnicity, nationality, religion, disability, or another protected characteristic.
- The product must not label one identity as “normal,” expose why a person was excluded, or impose weaker privacy defaults based on gender.

## Filters and compatibility

The product may support private, self-reported compatibility preferences such as activity level, lifestyle, communication, relationship style, intimacy interests, grooming, fragrance, and distance bands.

The product must not rank or filter by race, ethnicity, skin color, disability, or photograph-inferred protected traits. It must not infer intelligence, hygiene, sexuality, gender, fitness, grooming, or body hair from images.

## Reporting, blocking, and appeals

- Blocking and reporting are free and available without a paid plan.
- Blocking must suppress future discovery, proximity disclosure, messaging, group access, and active location grants as quickly as technically possible.
- Automation may prioritize, rate-limit, or temporarily contain urgent risk.
- Permanent bans and similarly consequential actions require policy-based **human review**, except where an immediate legal or security block is mandatory.
- Appeals use the in-product or published channel; bad-faith automated or abusive mass appeals may be limited.

## Enforcement

Responses may include warning, profile/content limits, proximity or location disablement, marketplace removal, creator payout hold, temporary containment, account suspension, permanent ban, evidence preservation, and referral to appropriate authorities when legally required.

Decentralization does **not** remove operator duties for discovery, distribution, payments, safety systems, or services the operator controls.

## Deletion and decentralization limits

- You may delete your account and request deletion of operator-held data under the privacy notice.
- Cryptographic and peer limits: the operator cannot force an untrusted peer to erase screenshots or plaintext already received.
- Location grants, proximity identifiers, mailbox ciphertext, purchase records, report evidence, legal holds, and fraud records have separate purpose-limited retention rules.
- Public Skin Shop assets and transaction records may need different deletion treatment from private dating content.

## Safety limitations (plain language)

Safety tools reduce risk but **cannot** guarantee identity, detect every nearby phone, prevent screenshots, erase all remote copies, ensure police response, prove compatibility, or make an in-person meeting safe.
